Lézard

If the collapse of civilization is inevitable, it might as well happen to a fierce groove. The Ghent-based collective Lézard realized this early on. Beneath their stylish, geometric post-punk sound simmers a hysterical energy, a kind of Boogie Wonderland for eccentrics, misfits and lost souls, where everyone is welcome.

Drifting in the spirit of Devo, LCD Soundsystem, Talking Heads and Telex, Lézard’s ‘disco vogue’ art-punk escapades have already drawn plenty of attention. The group wears catchiness like a three-piece suit that’s just a little too tight on a sweltering summer day: sooner or later, the figurative tie comes loose. On stage, Lézard escalates in the most bewildering and joyful ways imaginable, amplified by the feverish audiovisual creations of Joannes Meulewaeter.

OLGA

OLGA feels like she walked straight out of a David Lynch film and has been crafting minimal electronic darkpop from her bedroom ever since. With pulsing beats and dreamy soundscapes, she gives shape to fears and desires in a world that feels both grounded and dystopian. Her background in disorienting drama films has made her a specialist in the art of the danceable identity crisis. After countless hours staring at the ceiling, she released her debut EP 'My Feet Are Wet', followed by two enchanting singles.
